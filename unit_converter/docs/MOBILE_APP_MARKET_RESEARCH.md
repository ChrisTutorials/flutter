## Part 1: What Actually Makes Money for Small Devs

### Revenue Model Comparison

| Model | Small Dev Viability | Realistic Revenue | Notes |
|-------|---------------------|-------------------|-------|
| **Paid App** | ✅ Good | $500-2K/month at 1K-5K downloads | Needs unique value |
| **Freemium** | ⚠️ Hard | $100-500/month | Requires large volume |
| **Subscription** | ✅ Good | $500-5K/month | Must deliver ongoing value |
| **Rewarded Ads** | ✅ Good | $200-1K/month | Users opt-in, better UX |
| **Affiliate** | ✅ Viable | $50-500/month | Low overhead |

**For limited ad budget:** Focus on **paid apps** or **rewarded ads**. Avoid interstitial-heavy ad models.

### What Successful Indie Apps Have in Common

1. **Solves ONE problem completely** - Not 10 features, but 1 feature done better than anyone
2. **Passionate community** - Built audience before/during launch
3. **Excellent reviews** - 4.5+ stars from 100+ reviews minimum
4. **Regular updates** - Shows maintenance
5. **Niche keyword ranking** - Found via ASO, not ads

### Success Stories

| App | Developer | Model | Keys to Success |
|-----|-----------|-------|-----------------|
| PCalc | James Thomson | Paid ($9.99) | Best scientific calc, passionate users |

## Part 2: Game Utility Apps - Analysis

### Why Game Utilities Are Promising

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| **Competition** | Low - big apps ignore niches |
| **Community** | Engaged users who WANT tools |
| **Monetization** | Users habitually pay for game advantages |
| **Updates** | Game evolves = ongoing app value |

### Game Utility Categories That Work

**1. Log/State Trackers** ✅
- Track complex game state (like Town of Salem)
- Examples needed: D&D campaign trackers, card game logs
- Monetization: Paid ($1-5) or freemium

**2. Companion Apps** ✅
- Unofficial companions for specific games
- Examples: D&D spell cards, resource planners
- Monetization: Paid + ads for free version

**3. Data/Resource Trackers** ✅
- Idle game progress trackers
- Inventory managers
- Achievement trackers
- Monetization: Affiliate + rewarded ads

**4. Wiki/Guide Apps** ⚠️
- Offline game wikis
- Competition from official wikis
- Need unique UX to compete

### Town of Salem Specifically

| Opportunity | Assessment |
|------------|------------|
| Competition | **Zero** - no dedicated apps exist |
| Community | Active Reddit/Discord (12+ years) |
| Game Complexity | High - lots to track |
| Developer Effort | Medium - 2-4 weeks MVP |
| Monetization | Paid ($1-3) or freemium |

## Part 3: Underserved Niches (Beyond Games)

### Viable Categories for Small Devs

**1. Niche Productivity**
- Medical note-taking (not general - specific to specialties)
- Trade-specific tools (electricians, plumbers)
- Industry calculators

**2. Collection Managers**
- Vinyl record collection
- Comic book collection
- Coin/stamp collection
- These users are passionate and willing to pay

**3. Widget Apps**
- Not general (already dominated)
- Very specific: "Prayer time widget", "Crypto price widget"

**4. Accessibility Tools**
- Screen readers for specific needs
- Accessibility utilities
- Big apps ignore edge cases

**5. Localization Tools**
- Apps for specific language communities
- Translation utilities for niche languages

### Niches to AVOID

| Category | Why |
|----------|-----|
| Generic note-taking | Notion, Bear dominate |
| Basic calculators | Already free everywhere |
| Standard weather | Built into OS |
| Generic to-do | Todoist, Things dominate |
| General social | Facebook, Twitter own |
