### Root Cause (CORRECTED)

**The problem is NOT bad ratings - it's ZERO visibility.**

- App released into saturated market with no marketing push
- No reviews = no social proof = algorithm buries it
- Title "Unit Converter" is generic and competes with 500+ apps
- No differentiation in title, description, or screenshots

**Score: 7/12** - Core features solid, but missing expansion categories

### Why Zero Downloads (Actual Root Cause)

Based on app behavior and market patterns:

1. **Generic title** - "Unit Converter" doesn't stand out among 500+ competitors
2. **No promo video** - Competitors have videos, this doesn't
3. **No reviews** - Algorithm starts with zero visibility
4. **"Contains ads" label** - Google shows this prominently, users filter out

### Growth Effort Analysis

| Action | Effort | Impact | Timeline |
|--------|--------|--------|----------|
| Fix ASO (title, desc, screenshots) | Low | Medium | 1 week |
| In-app review prompt | Medium | High | 1-2 weeks |
| Reduce ad frequency | Low | High | 1 day |
| Add new unit categories | High | Medium | 1 month |
| Build separate paid app | High | High | 1 month |

**Verdict:** Recovery is possible but requires sustained effort and potentially a separate clean-slate paid app.

## Part 4: Recommendation

### Strategic Direction

**Pivot to Town of Salem Log Generator** as primary focus.

**Reasoning:**

1. **Market timing** - Unit converter market is mature, rating recovery is slow and uncertain. Town of Salem has ZERO competition.

2. **Effort vs Reward** - 2-4 weeks for MVP vs 3-6 months for unit converter recovery. Faster feedback loop.

3. **Clean Slate Psychology** - Starting fresh with no 1★ rating is fundamentally different than recovering from one.

4. **Niche Dominance** - Being #1 in a small niche often beats being #50 in a big category.

### If Staying With Unit Converter

The path forward requires:

1. **Immediate (1.0.6):** ASO fixes + ad reduction (don't touch code without testing)
2. **Short-term (1.0.7):** In-app review prompts
3. **Mid-term (1.1.0):** Paid "Pro" variant as separate listing

**Without** a paid variant or radical ad reduction, the app will continue to sit at 1★.

### If Pivoting to Town of Salem

**MVP Scope:**
- Night log entry (who visited whom)
- Day voting tracker
- Basic will generator
- 3-5 role templates
- Local save/export

**Skip for MVP:**
- Cloud sync
- Social sharing
- Achievement tracking
- Premium unlock

## Conclusion

**The unit converter problem is a visibility problem, not a reputation problem.**

With ~0 downloads and no reviews, this is actually **easier to fix** than recovering from bad ratings. The app:

- Has solid features (Custom Units, 150+ currencies, themes)
- Has NO rating damage
- Just needs ASO help + time to build reviews

### If Staying With Unit Converter

**This is a valid path.** The app isn't broken - it just can't be found.

1. **1.0.6 (ASO):** Title, description, screenshots fixes → submit
2. **1.0.7 (Marketing):** Ask friends/family to download and review
3. **1.0.8 (Features):** Add a missing category (fuel economy) to refresh store listing
4. **Monitor:** Watch downloads improve over 3-6 months

**Realistic trajectory:** 6-12 months to reach 100-500 downloads/month in this category.

### If Pivoting to Town of Salem

**Also valid** - but different strategy. First-mover in an uncontested niche vs fighting for visibility in a mature category.

**Town of Salem advantage:** Could become #1 in that niche in weeks.  
**Town of Salem disadvantage:** Smaller total market, uncertain revenue.

### Final Recommendation

**Stay with unit converter** if you want:
- Steady, predictable growth
- Utility app market (stable but not exciting)
- Existing codebase investment

**Pivot to Town of Salem** if you want:
- Faster initial traction
- First-mover advantage
- Game helper niche (more interesting than utilities)

**Both is possible** - unit converter needs only occasional ASO updates, Town of Salem could be the new primary focus.
